About Beem

Beem Wilder

Ph.D. – My background as a Meditation Teacher and Psychologist

In the early 1970’s I left my home in Los Gatos California and traveled around the world for two years, mostly overland, and started my meditation and yoga practice in a small yoga ashram in Rishikesh India. After returning home I was initiated by Bhagavan Nityananda and I traveled to Ganeshpuri India and became a student of Nityananda’s disciple, Swami Muktananda. I stayed with Swami Muktananda for 12 years and was often the temple drummer during chanting (kirtan) and was his personal driver for several years. In the early 1990’s I practiced and trained with Gen Khandro in the Tibetan Buddhist tradition for five years and was authorized to teach meditation and trainings. Since 2000 I have been a student of Thich Nhat Hanh in the Zen tradition and was ordained in the Order of Interbeing (OI) in 2012. My wife Sally and I have had a meditation center in our home for the last 20 years and host monthly daylong practice sessions for other OI members. As a child psychologist at Kaiser Permanente, I started a family meditation program and also taught patients and staff meditation in the hospital until my retirement from Kaiser three years ago. As a meditation teacher I honor and incorporate teachings from many world traditions and especially the Zen Buddhist tradition and the teachings of Ramana Maharshi and Sri Ramakrishna. I provide consultation and trainings in mindfulness and meditation and teach weekly meditation classes in several locations and also teach in the business and medical settings.

I am profoundly aware of the power of spiritual practices such as yoga and meditation to transform negative mental states and to create a path of lifelong healing and healthy self-actualization. The core teaching is, “We already have what we are looking for- it is just covered up”. Spiritual practice lets the covering dissolve so our true light of being can shine out.

In my personal life I am a father with two grown children.  I have travelled extensively and lived outside of the United States including five years living in India.  I can converse and read fairly well in Spanish and am studying so that I can teach meditation in Spanish. I enjoy surfing, biking, riding my one-wheel pint, meditating, creating mosaics,and hiking with my wife Sally. Sally and I have been practicing meditation together for almost 50 years and she and I often co-facilitate meditation trainings and classes.

As a clinical psychologist I see clients in my office in Los Gatos working with individuals, families, teens and young adults.
