Beginning Anew


When communication breaks down between individuals and there is hurt and misunderstanding in the relationship, applying an approach called “Beginning Anew” may be helpful.

This practice is used in the Buddhist community to create a fresh understanding about the communication breakdown and hopefully lead to reconciliation. Beginning Anew begins with a request from the person who feels the hurt or suffering (we will call him “Juan”) to the person that Juan believes is causing the suffering (we will call her “Julia”), “Will you please practice Beginning Anew with me?” If Julia agrees, the practice will unfold in four steps. Julia will simply listen to Juan without responding and if she feels uncomfortable or unsafe, she can stop the practice at any time by ringing a bell.

  1. The first step is called “Watering the Flower. Juan looks deeply in himself to find 3 or 4 things about Julia that he truly appreciates such as helpful things she has done in the past or good qualities that she has. When Juan sincerely looks at Julia with eyes of appreciation, already the hurt he carries begins to lessen.
  2. In the second step, Juan will express regret for anything that he may have done or said that is contributing to the current difficulty. This step is very important and Juan needs to be very honest about his short-comings and lack of mindful speech.
  3. The third step is about Juan expressing his hurt to Julia using “I” statements and loving speech. Juan does not blame or argue, but is clear so that everything is said and is out in the open.
  4. A fourth step may be used when Juan expresses the wish that Julia help him to create healthy communication moving forward.
During Beginning Anew Julia simply listens. If she wants to do Beginning Anew with Juan, she will make the request to do so and the process is reversed with Juan listening. From my experience, usually only the first two steps of Beginning Anew are needed. By the time we really reflect deeply about the other person’s good qualities and how we have contributed to the situation, our hurt has transformed into understanding and a new perspective.
